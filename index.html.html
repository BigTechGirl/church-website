A Word From The President

The President's Word

"Welcome to Youth Week 2026 — our theme this year is one word: Explore."

With great joy and gratitude to God, I welcome you to another remarkable Youth Week. This is the second time I will be leading this celebration as your Youth President — and the older this responsibility gets, the more I feel both the weight and the joy of it.

Each year, this week is set apart — to gather in spirit, share fellowship, study God's word afresh, and grow deeper in our commitment to Christ and to one another. This year God has placed before us the limitless opportunities of exploration — it is about discovering who God created us to be, and how we can impact our world for Christ.

I bring you warm greetings from the National President of the Nigerian Baptist Convention Youth Conference, Ambassador Amos Barnabas Kadanga. Let us explore together. The land is waiting.

Princess Jemimah Adetula

The Theme

What Does It Mean To Explore?

Exploration isn't aimless wandering. It's the purposeful decision to lift your eyes above your current situation and step into what God has already promised. Abram didn't know the details — he just took the first step.

"Look around from where you are, to the north and south, to the east and west. All the land that you see I will give to you and your offspring forever."

Genesis 13:14–15
👁️
Lift Your Eyes
Stop looking at your limitations. Look at your potential. God sees the whole land — let Him show you.
👣
Walk Through It
Faith without action is incomplete. Abram had to walk the land to claim it. So do you.
🔥
Step Out
Comfort zones are comfortable but they're not promised land. This week we step out together.
🌍
Explore Everything
Your faith, your purpose, your relationships, your finances, your future. All of it is land to explore.

Walk Through The Land
Joshua 1:3

"Before you can explore the land, you have to prepare your heart to receive it."

The week before Youth Week is not downtime — it's your runway. Every morning from June 1st, we pray together as a community, each day building toward Youth Week's opening on June 7th.

You don't have to be in the same room. You just have to show up — wherever you are — and lift your eyes before the day begins.

How it works
  • Join the FBC Youth WhatsApp group
  • Each morning at 6:00 AM, receive your daily theme, scripture, and 3 prayer points
  • Spend 10 minutes in prayer before your day starts
  • Fast however you are led — a meal, social media, or a habit
  • Arrive at Youth Week already prepared
